Summer Olympics of 1896-2024.

About the Olympics

Originating in 1896 and being one of the most prestigious all-round sporting events, the Olympic is one of the most watched events throughout the world. The summer Olympics, synonymously called the Olympics due its dominance in popularity over the winter Olympics, contains 206 nations, containing a National Olympic Committee. Being held every 4 years and achieving an estimated audience of over 3 billion people for the previous Olympics in Tokyo, there are scarcely any achievements which can compare to winning a gold medal within the Olympics.

Olympic Locations

The above map displays the locations of every city which hosted a Summer Olympic Games. The circles on the map act as a marker for the cities in which the Olympics were held. The colour of the marker represents the continent of the city with the size demonstrating the number of times the games has been hosted between 1896-2024, inclusive. Note, that throughout these years the Olympic games for 1916, 1940 and 1944 were cancelled due to World Wars, hence these years are not included. Hovering over the circles will display the name of each city as well as the most recent year in which that city hosted an Olympic Games.

The first modern Olympic Games was held in Athens, Greece due to the original Olympic Games originating from Greece in 776BC. Following the return of the modern Olympics, Paris, Los Angeles and London all have the highest number of Olympic Games held, with each city hosting 3 times.

Therefore, Europe has hosted the most Olympic Games out of all the continents, hosting a sum of 15 summer Olympics. Contrastingly, Africa has yet to host an Olympic games as is the only habitable continent which has not had a summer Olympics, with the second least being held in South America, which has only hosted 1 summer Olympics.

National Pride

In order for a country to compete in the Olympics as a 'nation,' they must have a National Olympic Committee. With 206 recognized nations participating in the Olympic Games, there are more nations competing within the Olympics than there are recognized nations in the world (195).

With athletes wearing national garments, singing national anthems, and competing within national teams for certain events, the Olympics is very much a competition between nations where athletes represent the pride and skill of their country.

On the left, the stacked bar chart visualizes the top 15 nations in regard to combined medals achieved. This bar chart stacked the bronze, gold and silver medals in that order and ranks the countries by their total accumulative medals. Currently USA contains the most total medals by a significant margin. In fact, the gold medals of Soviet Union alone are larger than the combined medals earned by the second highest nation, Britain. Hovering over the bars will clarify the number of gold, silver, and bronze medals won by each country.

Below this stacked bar chart is a vertical bar chart with the number of participation of these same 15 countries in the Olympics. Note that despite being the 2nd largest medal winners, the USA has notably less participation than other top medal winners such as Soviet Union and Britain. However, due to the monumental difference in achieved medals, the USA still dominates the number of medals won per participation at the summer Olympics.

Personal Victory

The Olympics can be regarded as the pinnacle of international competition. While making it into the Olympics alone is an amazing feat, wining an event will regard you as the greatest athlete within that category. A gold medal, made primarily of silver with a gold coating, can be evaluated to cost anywhere between $20000-$50000.
